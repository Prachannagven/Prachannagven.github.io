# [Minimal Library Sensor Control](https://github.com/Prachannagven/Arduino_BareMetal_Code)

In the winter break of my first year in college, (perhaps inspired by my recent obsession with [Ben Eater](https://www.youtube.com/@BenEater)), I decided that I didn't know enough about microcontrollers, and their surrounding sensors. So, I decided to crack open my big-ol' Arduino sensors kit, and start playing around.

My goal was to write code for all the sensors, without using their nice libraries that other people had already written. That meant, for everything from a temperature sensor to an RTC and even an I2C OLED display, I wrote device-level code.

It took quite a bit of reading of the datasheets, and a lot more of trial-and-error, but eventually I had a pretty decent collection of peripherals that I could control without any additional libraries (save for the I2C library, I'll get to that in a bit).
